About

I am currently a third year PhD candidate at the University of Maryland, where I study auditory perceptual learning in the Caras Lab. Broadly, I am interested in the neurocircuitry underlying communication behaviors from a neuroethological point of view. In the Caras lab, I've been researching the role of subcortical areas in auditory perceptual learning.

Previously, I worked at UNC Chapel Hill and received my undergraduate degree from Wake Forest University. You can view my CV here.

About

I am currently a third year graduate student at the University of Maryland, where I study auditory perceptual learning in the Caras Lab. Broadly, I am interested in the neurocircuitry underlying communication behaviors from a neuroethological point of view.

Current Projects

I am currently investigating the role of corticofugal projections and subcortical areas in perceptual learning.

Corticofugal signalling

I am interested in extratelencephalic corticofugal projections originating in layer 5 of the auditory cortex and what kind of information they are sending to subcortical regions during perceptual learning. To investigate these questions, I am using intersectional viral strategies to target this specific projection and using projection-specific fiber photometry to record calcium transients.

Subcortical plasticity

Subcortical auditory areas, like the medial geniculate nucleus and inferior colliculus, display plasticity during learning. I use 64 channel electrophysiology in awake, behaving setups to ask questions about how plasticity arises in these subcortical areas during perceptual learning.

Orbitofrontal cortex and its connections to auditory regions

Orbitofrontal cortex is implicated in sensory learning and decision-making. I use viral tracing and confocal imaging techniques to determine how this higher-order area connects to auditory regions of interest.
